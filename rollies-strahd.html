Where superstition meets statistics — intervals, priors, and the honest truth of small samples
Crit & Fumble Rates — Wilson 95% Intervals
Each soul's rate of the chosen omen, with Wilson score intervals. The golden line marks the fair-d20 null (5%). When a player's entire interval clears the line, the dice are significantly blessed or cursed — not merely whispered about. Intervals widen honestly for those with fewer trials.
The True Measure — Bayesian Posterior Means
Each player's true fortune, drawn as a posterior shrunk toward the group's prior. Filled marks are posterior means; bars are 95% credible intervals; hollow marks show the raw sample mean. The mists pull the under-sampled toward the center — no soul is crowned luckiest on two fevered sessions alone.
